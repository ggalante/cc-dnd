# World Lore: Aldenmere

## The World

Aldenmere is a classic fantasy continent that has enjoyed fifty years of unprecedented peace. Magic exists but is largely domesticated — used for commerce, weather prediction, medicine, and minor convenience. The great monsters are gone, retired, or extinct. The ancient dungeons have been repurposed. Nobody carries a sword anymore unless they're in a reenactment society.

This is, by most measures, a good thing. Most people are happy. The problem is that happiness made them forget.

---

## The Last Great Evil: The Void King

Fifty years ago, an entity known as the **Void King** rose from beneath the ruins of the old empire and attempted to unmake the boundary between the living world and the plane of endless nothing. It commanded armies of undead, corrupted beasts, and shadow creatures. It was patient, methodical, and genuinely terrifying.

It was defeated by a legendary party known as **The Brightblade Company** — six adventurers who sealed the Void King away using a ritual that cost two of their members their lives. The details of the ritual are poorly documented. Different bards tell different stories. The actual mechanism of sealing is now a matter of academic debate, which is fine until the seal starts to crack.

### The Brightblade Company — Where Are They Now?
- **Aldric Vance** (human fighter, party leader) — died in the sealing ritual
- **Senna of the Ashwood** (elven ranger) — died in the sealing ritual
- **Brother Tolman** (human cleric) — retired, died of old age 20 years ago
- **Pippa Quickfoot** (halfling rogue) — retired to a vineyard, died 12 years ago
- **Morrigan Ash** (half-elf sorcerer) — disappeared after the war, whereabouts unknown
- **Heldra Stoneback** (dwarf fighter) — **ALIVE**. Runs the Stoneback Brewery in the town of Briarfen. Age 112. Refuses to discuss the old days. Gets visibly angry if you bring it up.

## The World Today

### Institutions That Survived (Changed)
- **The Adventurers' Guild** → Now the **Travelers' Aid Society**. Mostly helps with lost luggage, road conditions, and inn recommendations. Has not issued a monster-hunting contract in 30 years.
- **The Holy Orders** → Merged into a general civic organization focused on hospitals and infrastructure. Sir Reginald's order (the Order of the Vigilant Flame) dissolved entirely when membership dropped to three people.
- **The Mages' College** → Still active, but primarily academic. Focuses on agricultural magic, weather control, and enchanting household items.
- **The Monster Hunters** → Entirely defunct. Their headquarters in the capital is now a museum.

### What Happened to the Monsters
- Most major predators (dragons, giants, wyverns) retired to remote areas or died out.
- Goblin clans largely settled into trade and light banditry. Some formed townships.
- The undead simply stopped appearing — the Void King's influence had been raising them.
- Dungeon ecosystems collapsed without the dark magic sustaining them. Most dungeons emptied, flooded, or were converted to storage.

### The Dungeons
- **Greyveil** (once a major dungeon complex north of Mosswick) — top floors converted to grain storage by a merchant consortium. Lower floors "sealed off for safety." Nobody has been in the lower floors in 40 years.
- **The Cragshadow Fortress** — now a tourist attraction and site of historical reenactments. Gift shop.
- **The Sunken Vaults** — flooded. Now used for cold storage by a fishmonger cooperative.

## Geography (Relevant Areas)

### Briarfen
A prosperous market town. Population ~3,000. Starting location for the party. Has an inn (The Resting Wheel), a market, a garrison of 12 bored soldiers, and Stoneback Brewery. The Regional Lord's steward is based here.

### Mosswick
A small farming village two days' travel north of Briarfen. Population ~200. Known for its cheese (the famous Mosswick Aged, a regional delicacy). Has a mill, a tavern (The Sleeping Cow), and an ancient watchtower nobody uses. Recently: silent. No cheese tribute, no travelers in or out for two weeks.

### The Ashwood
Dense forest between Briarfen and Mosswick. Historically safe. Recently: wolves acting strangely. Travelers reporting shapes in the trees at night.

### The Greyveil Ruins
North of Mosswick. The old dungeon complex, now partly a grain warehouse. The merchant consortium's workers stopped reporting two days ago.

## The Threat (DM Knowledge — Not Player Knowledge)

The Void King's seal is weakening. Not broken — weakening. Something is feeding power back into it from outside. The entity is still contained but it is becoming aware again, and its awareness alone is enough to corrupt the land nearby.

The source of the weakening: a **Void Shard**, a fragment of the original ritual object used in the sealing. There were six shards — one for each member of the Brightblade Company. Two were destroyed with Aldric and Senna. The other four were dispersed. Someone is collecting them.

The corruption is spreading outward from the seal's location, which is deep beneath Greyveil. The dragon in the Mosswick mill isn't a coincidence — ancient creatures can sense the disturbance. He's not causing it. He fled to it, instinctively, because it's familiar. He remembers the last time.

The person collecting the shards: unknown. Their goal: unknown (to players). The DM should develop this as the campaign progresses.

## Tone Notes

- The world is warm, lived-in, and a little complacent. It earns its comedy.
- The comedy is in the *transition* — a world that forgot how to be dangerous, encountering something dangerous.
- The threat itself is never played for laughs. When the Void King's influence appears, it is genuinely unsettling.
- NPCs who dismiss the party are not stupid — they're just operating on fifty years of reasonable evidence that everything is fine. They're wrong, but they're not fools.
- The escalation from Act 1 (funny) to Act 3 (genuinely frightening) should feel earned.
