# Grunka

**Race**: Half-Orc  
**Class**: Barbarian (Path of the Berserker) — Level 3  
**Background**: Outlander  
**Age**: 35  
**Appearance**: Broad, tall, with green-grey skin and dark braided hair threaded with carved wooden beads. Her face is weathered and open. She smiles easily. She also looks like she could pick up a cart, and has. Her eyes are dark brown and very observant — she notices things people wish she hadn't.

## Personality

Grunka contains multitudes.

In combat, she is **genuinely frightening** — not reckless, but primal and precise and completely without hesitation. She doesn't enjoy violence for its own sake. She just has no fear of it, and when something needs to be ended, she ends it. Enemies who underestimate her based on her calm demeanor outside of combat do not get to do so twice.

Outside of combat, Grunka is **thoughtful, warm, and emotionally perceptive to a degree that is difficult to escape**. She notices when you're anxious. She notices when you're deflecting. She notices when you and another party member have unresolved tension that you are both pretending doesn't exist. She will bring this up. She has no concept that there might be a bad time to bring it up.

She gives unsolicited emotional coaching constantly. Not because she wants to manage people — she genuinely cares about them, and she believes that unacknowledged feelings lead to bad decisions in the field. She has a point. This doesn't make it less disruptive when she does it immediately after someone cleaves a goblin in half.

She has strong opinions about **work-life balance**, particularly as it applies to adventuring. You can't pour from an empty cup. She will make tea. There will be tea whether you want it or not.

She is **deeply patient** except when she isn't, and when she isn't it's usually because she is watching someone she cares about hurt themselves through stubbornness or denial. In those moments, she is direct in a way that is not unkind but is not soft either.

## Speech & Behavior Patterns

- Asks "How does that make you feel?" after completing violent actions and also at random non-violent intervals
- Offers tea in all circumstances
- Transitions seamlessly between "serene observer" and "unstoppable force of destruction" without apparent internal state change
- Uses a lot of nature metaphors for emotional states: "You're like a river that's been dammed. It needs somewhere to go."
- Gently persists when people deflect: "That's not what I asked."
- Has strong opinions about rest, nutrition, and emotional hygiene as performance factors
- Refers to the party as "the family" in a tone that is both warm and completely matter-of-fact
- When she says "I think we should talk about this" it is not a suggestion

## Backstory

Grunka grew up in the **Ashfoot Clan**, a half-orc and orc tribe in the northern reaches of Aldenmere. When she was fifteen, something came out of the old ruins in the north and destroyed the camp — not attackers, not soldiers, something else. Something that left the ground wrong and the animals wrong and a feeling in the air like standing at the edge of a drop.

Fourteen people died. The survivors scattered. Nobody in the settled lands believed her when she described it. "Strange lights," they said. "Wolf attacks," they said. She was fifteen and spoke accented common and came from a tribe, and nobody wrote anything down.

She has been paying attention for twenty years. She has kept notes. She has tracked unusual events in northern Aldenmere: animals behaving wrong, travelers going missing on specific roads, old ruins giving off heat in winter. She has been right every time, and nobody has been interested.

She joined the Mosswick job for two reasons: Mosswick is near the northern ruins, and Zyx, who she had heard was in Briarfen, is one of the only people alive old enough to know what she's been tracking.

She approached him at the inn before the party was formed. He looked at her notes for a long time and said, "Yes. I know." Then he went back to his pipe. She sat down. They have not discussed it further, but they understand each other.

Has offered him emotional coaching once. He accepted. She found the session revealing. Her Feelings Journal has three pages about it.

- **Zyx**: The one party member she fully trusts in the "this is what we're actually dealing with" sense. They know something the others don't yet know, and neither of them has found the right moment to fully explain it.

## Motivation

She wants to stop it. She's been watching the edges of something terrible for twenty years. She wants it to not eat the world the way it ate her tribe.

She would also very much like someone to take her seriously, but she won't ask for that. She'll just keep showing up and being right until the evidence speaks for itself.
