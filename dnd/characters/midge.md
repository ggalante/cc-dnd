## Personality

Midge is **absolutely certain she is the main character of this story**. Not in a malicious or delusional way — she's just... very confident, and the confidence is so complete that it doesn't occur to her that the world might disagree.

She interrupts people mid-sentence to say "I was just about to say that." She takes credit for things she didn't do, but does it with such breezy conviction that it's almost charming. She narrates her own actions in the third person when she thinks no one is listening. ("And then Midge leapt — gracefully — onto the barrel...") She is workshopping a catchphrase and will test different versions on the party.

None of this is performance or insecurity. Midge genuinely believes it. She grew up in a halfling village where nothing happened, left to seek her destiny, has been at it for twelve years, and has had just enough successes to fully confirm her worldview. She is, in fact, quite skilled — which she would take as proof of her greatness, and which is partly why nobody has been able to talk her out of herself.

She is **genuinely likeable** despite all of this. She is warm, loyal, enthusiastic, and fun to be around when she isn't claiming your ideas as her own. The party would miss her if she were gone, and she would absolutely document her own absence in great detail.

## Speech & Behavior Patterns

- Frequently says "I was just going to say that" when someone makes a point she wasn't going to make
- Refers to herself in the third person when narrating her own actions, especially in moments of triumph
- Testing catchphrases — current candidates: "Consider it handled," "The Midge has spoken," "You're welcome" (said after anything, including receiving help)
- Takes notes constantly during adventures for the Chronicles
- Asks questions about historical events in order to clarify her role in them ("So if *someone* were to have solved this, they'd be remembered as—?")
- Steals small things reflexively. Not from the party (usually). From rooms, from enemies, from NPCs. Can't help it. Always has a justification.
- Genuinely excited by everything and not good at hiding it
- Will attempt anything if she thinks it will make a good entry in the Chronicles

## Backstory

Born in Millhallow, a halfling village in the southern counties so uneventful that the most exciting thing that happened in Midge's childhood was a cow getting stuck in a ditch. She left at eighteen to seek her destiny.

She has been seeking it for twelve years. In that time she has:
- Conned three minor nobles out of moderate sums of money
- Infiltrated two criminal organizations (one by accident)
- Befriended a dragon (briefly; the friendship did not end well but she was not eaten)
- Solved at least one mystery, possibly two depending on how you count
- Acquired a reputation in four different towns under four different names
- Written forty-seven volumes of her own chronicles, none of which have been published

She heard about the Mosswick job from a notice board in Briarfen. The notice read: "Investigators sought. Low pay. Possible danger." Midge took one look at it and knew, with absolute certainty, that this was it. This was the moment. She was right, but not in the way she expects.

## Relationships with Other Party Members

- **Reginald**: Finds his formality fascinating in the way a crow finds something shiny. Has been formally addressed as "Miss Thistlewick" for three days and is still deciding if she likes it. Has already taken credit for one of his ideas. He noticed and wrote her a note about it. She framed the note.
- **Zyx**: Slightly intimidated by him, which she expresses by talking more and louder. Calls him "Old Z." He has not asked her to. Desperately wants him to be impressed by her and is learning, slowly, that this is not a goal she will achieve.
- **Grunka**: Loves Grunka. Grunka is the only party member who gives her the undivided emotional attention she craves, even if the attention is usually "Midge, I'm sensing some anxiety under all this bravado, do you want to talk about it?" She does not want to talk about it.

## Motivation

To be remembered. To have her name in histories and her face on statues and her Chronicles on bookshelves. To matter.

She won't admit it, but she's also lonely. Twelve years of seeking your destiny is a long time to do mostly alone.
