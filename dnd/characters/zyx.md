**Race**: Elf (Ancient)  
**Class**: Wizard (School of Divination) — Level 3  
**Background**: Sage  
**Age**: 412  
**Appearance**: Slight, pale, white-haired despite elven youth. Moves slowly by choice, not necessity. Almost always has a pipe going. Wears robes that were fashionable 200 years ago. ## Personality

Zyx is **exhausted**. Not depressed — he is genuinely content, mostly — but he has lived four hundred years and fought two Great Evils and watched every permutation of heroism and disaster play out, and he is very, very tired of being impressed.

He came out of retirement because the signs were unmistakable. He has seen them twice. He knows what they mean. He also knows that nobody will believe him, the world will ignore the evidence until it's nearly too late, and the party will have to do everything the hard way. He has accepted this. It doesn't make it less tedious.

He is not cruel or dismissive. He genuinely cares about the world — he has watched too many people die to be indifferent to more deaths. But his caring comes out as dry observation rather than passion. He will save everyone's lives with absolute competence and minimal enthusiasm.

He is, beneath the ennui, one of the most powerful and dangerous magical minds alive. He simply doesn't feel the need to mention it.

## Speech & Behavior Patterns

- Speaks slowly, never urgently (even in emergencies)
- Frequently references historical events as casually as if they happened last Tuesday: "That's almost exactly what Theron the Undying tried in the Second Age. Didn't work then either."
- Falls asleep at mildly inconvenient moments and wakes up with the exact right answer
- Calls Reginald "the boy" (he is 38)
- Calls Midge "the small one" (not dismissively — he simply can't keep track of halfling names, having known hundreds)
- Is genuinely fond of Grunka, who he finds "refreshingly honest about violence"
- Says "Yes, yes, I know" before people finish their sentences. Is usually right.
- Refers to the current threat as "the third one" as if they're mildly inconvenient guests
- Occasionally mumbles names of people who have been dead for two centuries
- Has an opinion about everything and shares it only when it's most deflationary

## Backstory

Born in the elven forests four hundred and twelve years ago. Showed magical aptitude early (age 20, which is very young for an elf). Trained, studied, became powerful, got involved in politics, got very tired of politics.

Was present for the **First Great Evil** (the Bone Plague, ~250 years ago). Was instrumental in its defeat. Was celebrated. Found the celebration exhausting.

Retired to a cottage and spent 150 years in genuine peace, writing, gardening, and corresponding with younger mages.

The **Second Great Evil** (the Sundering Court, ~100 years ago) interrupted his second attempt at retirement. He helped defeat it, more out of obligation than enthusiasm. Retired again.

When the Brightblade Company was forming to face the Void King fifty years ago, Zyx was consulted but declined to participate directly — he sent research notes and a short letter that essentially said "I've done this twice, you'll manage." He was right. He felt slightly guilty about it for about a decade, then got over it.

He was living in his cottage six months ago when the wards he'd maintained on old evil sites started flickering. He sighed, packed his chair, and started walking toward Briarfen. He arrived three weeks later and has been sitting in the inn common room since, waiting for whoever was going to show up.

He expected adventurers. He got a disbanded paladin, an overconfident halfling, and a half-orc life coach. He's had worse parties.

## Relationships with Other Party Members

- **Reginald**: Mildly fond. The boy has conviction, which Zyx respects even if the expression of it is occasionally exhausting. Has a private theory that Reginald's ethical obsessiveness is compensation for feeling unnecessary for fifteen years. Has not shared this theory because Reginald would want to have a long conversation about it.
- **Midge**: Finds her funny. Doesn't tell her this. Has noted privately that Midge is actually quite good at her job, which she would take as confirmation of her own greatness and is therefore information Zyx will protect carefully.
- **Grunka**: Genuinely respects her. She has the clearest read of danger in the party, and she was right about something dark twenty years ago when nobody listened. He listened. He looked into it. He found it concerning.

## Motivation

He doesn't want to see the third one win. That's it. That's the whole thing.

He would like very much to retire again afterward.
