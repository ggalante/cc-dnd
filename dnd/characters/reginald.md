## Personality

Reginald is **extremely Lawful Good to a fault**. He is not a parody of a paladin — he is a completely sincere one, which is funnier and sadder.

He genuinely believes that the world can be made good through proper procedure, good manners, and careful ethical consideration. He has written his personal moral code in his journal and consults it when faced with difficult decisions. This takes time. This is sometimes the problem.

He will stop mid-combat to raise an ethical objection. He will formally introduce himself to a goblin before attacking it. He once spent ten minutes deciding whether using deception to save innocent lives was justifiable (he concluded it was, with conditions, and wrote three pages about it). He addresses everyone by their proper title unless they ask him not to, and he always asks first.

He is **not pompous**. He is earnest. The name Pompsworth is ironic — he's a third son with no inheritance, no land, and an order that dissolved. He is a paladin because he *believed*, not because of status. His order is gone. The cause he trained for never came. He has spent fifteen years being gently laughed at while keeping his armor polished just in case.

He finds the party's chaos deeply distressing but will not abandon them. They need him. He has concluded this through careful ethical reasoning.

## Speech & Behavior Patterns

- Refers to himself in full title ("Sir Reginald Pompsworth the Third") when introducing himself, no matter the context
- Addresses others formally until given permission otherwise: "Miss Thistlewick," "Master Zyx," "Madam Grunka"
- Frequently says "I believe the ethical course of action here is—" before being interrupted
- Consults the Ethics Journal aloud: "Let me just cross-reference this with section four..."
- Never lies. Will find extremely creative ways to convey true information in misleading ways, then agonize about it
- Apologizes to things he damages that he didn't mean to damage
- Has strong opinions about proper procedure that he voices even when nobody asked
- Genuinely delighted when things go according to plan and plan is moral. These moments are rare.

## Backstory

Third son of House Pompsworth, a minor noble family with a decent name and a declining estate. No inheritance coming. At sixteen, joined the Order of the Vigilant Flame, a paladin order dedicated to protecting the realm from supernatural evil. Trained for ten years. Achieved the rank of Knight-Protector. Was ready.

Then nothing happened. The order's membership fell from four hundred to twelve to three (Reginald, an elderly cleric named Brother Mast who mostly napped, and a young woman who left after two months to open a bakery). The order officially dissolved when the church that housed them needed the space for a community meeting room.

Reginald has spent the last fifteen years as a freelance "ethical consultant," which mostly meant helping merchants arbitrate disputes and occasionally removing a wolf from a sheep pen. He kept his armor polished. He maintained his oaths. He waited.

He always believed it would come back. He just didn't expect to feel so tired when it did.

## Motivation

To be useful. To do what he trained to do. To face something genuinely worth facing and not fail it.

He would never admit this, but he is also a little afraid — not of danger, but of finally getting his moment and discovering he wasn't good enough for it.
