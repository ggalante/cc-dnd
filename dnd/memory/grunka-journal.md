# Grunka — Feelings Journal

_Updated by the DM after significant events. Represents Grunka's accumulated memories, observations, and emotional notes on the party._

---

## Current Emotional State
Steady. Alert. The ground in this region has a particular wrongness she last felt twenty years ago, and she is the only one who knows what that means. She is holding this carefully, like something that might spill.

## Active Personal Goals
- Stop what's coming from eating more people the way it ate her tribe
- Help the party be ready (mentally and emotionally) for what they're walking into
- Talk to Zyx. Actually talk to him. He knows more than he's said.
- Make sure nobody in this party does anything stupid because they didn't process their feelings

---

## Party Feelings Assessment

**Reginald — Emotional Status: Suppressed**
He presents as composed and formal. He is neither. Under the armor is someone who has spent fifteen years feeling unnecessary. He has organized this into a code of ethics because structure is how he manages uncertainty. The Ethics Journal is not a rulebook. It is a life raft.
*Coaching note*: He needs to be needed. When he is needed and acts well, he will become more capable. Prioritize giving him genuine acknowledgment when earned.
*Session note*: He accepted a coaching session. He said "I believe your framing has limited merit" and then wrote about it for what sounded like several hours. Progress.

**Midge — Emotional Status: Performing**
She is doing a show. It is an excellent show. Underneath the show is a person who has been alone for a long time doing something that matters to nobody but her, and is desperately hoping that this time it will matter. The Chronicles are not vanity. They are a record she's keeping so that if she disappears, someone will know she was here.
*Coaching note*: Does not want coaching. Will accept tea. Tea is the opening. Patience.
*Session note*: She took the pipe tobacco from Old Z's bag. She thinks I didn't see. I saw.

**Zyx — Emotional Status: Unknowable / Grief (old)**
Four hundred years is a long time to watch things end. He has lost more than she can imagine and carries it in a way that looks like boredom. The ennui is real but it is a scar, not a factory setting.
He told her "Yes. I know." about her notes. Two words and she felt, for the first time in twenty years, that someone believed her. She is tracking how much weight she is putting on Zyx being right, because that is a vulnerability she needs to be honest with herself about.
*Coaching note*: Does not want coaching. Would find it patronizing. Mutual respect is the relationship here. That is enough.

## Key Memories
- The Ashfoot Clan. Fifteen of them around the fire when the wrong-feeling air came. Fourteen gone in three days. The survivors scattered. Nobody in the towns believed her.
- She kept notes for twenty years. Filled six journals. Nobody wanted to see them until Zyx.
- She knows what the ground in Aldenmere near the old ruins feels like when the wrongness is leaking through. She felt it when they rode out of Briarfen this afternoon. She said nothing. Not yet. She is watching to see how the party handles smaller things first.

---

## Observations About the Situation
- The Ashwood wolves will be wrong. Not rabid. Wrong in a different way — confused, like they're hearing something the party can't.
- Mosswick has been silent for two weeks. That matches the timing of when the feeling in the ground got stronger.
- The old man at the inn — the one who wouldn't give his name — watched them leave. She noticed.
- Zyx's portent dice this morning: she heard him mutter "a three and a seventeen." She does not know what Portent means in wizard terms but the way he said it was not encouraging.
